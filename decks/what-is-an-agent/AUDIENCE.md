# Deck Context

## Event

**AI & Society** meetup -- a national community exploring how AI is shaping lives, work, cities, and institutions.

## Audience

Mixed, non-technical. Engineers, business leaders, students, policymakers, creatives, educators, community members. No technical background required. People who want to understand AI but are not building it.

## Talk goal

Give the audience a shared mental model for what an agent actually is -- not to teach them to build one, but so they can think critically about agents they encounter, ask better questions, and participate in informed conversation about AI's role in society.

## Tone

Accessible. Concrete examples that don't require coding knowledge. Avoid jargon. The audience is curious and engaged, not sceptical -- but they need relatable anchors, not developer tooling examples.

## Notes

- The original opening/closing example (Claude Code editing files, running tests, opening a PR) is too developer-centric for this audience.
- The Lethal Trifecta slide uses the word "harness" and "prompt injection" -- may need plain-language alternatives.
